## 1. BREATHING TECHNIQUES

### 1.1 Cyclic Sighing (Physiological Sigh) - HIGHEST EVIDENCE

**Study:** Balban et al., Cell Reports Medicine, 2023
**Design:** RCT, n=111, 28 days, 5 min/day
**Result:** Greater mood improvement vs mindfulness (p<0.05)
**Source:** [Cell Reports Medicine](<https://www.cell.com/cell-reports-medicine/fulltext/S2666-3791(22)00474-8>)

### 1.2 Coherent Breathing (5-5, 0.1 Hz) - HIGH EVIDENCE

**Meta-analysis:** 172 studies analyzed
**Result:** Significant HRV increases, baroreflex resonance
**Source:** [Neuroscience Reviews](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0149763422002007)

### 1.3 Box Breathing (4-4-4-4) - MODERATE EVIDENCE

**Status:** Popular but limited RCT evidence
**Finding:** 6 bpm may be more effective for HRV
**Source:** [PLOS ONE](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0336615)

### 1.4 4-7-8 Breathing - MODERATE EVIDENCE

**RCT:** n=90, bariatric patients
**Result:** Reduced anxiety, improved QoL
**Source:** [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36480101/)

### 1.5 Bhramari (Humming) - MODERATE EVIDENCE

**Review:** 46 studies (6 RCTs)
**Result:** Higher parasympathetic activity, effect size d=1.7
**Source:** [Indian J Physiology](https://ijpp.com/exploring-the-health-benefits-of-bhramari-pranayama-humming-bee-breathing-a-comprehensive-literature-review/)

### 1.6 Alternate Nostril - MODERATE EVIDENCE

**Limitation:** Lacks standard technique, most RCTs focus on BP/HRV
**Source:** [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11996816/)

## 2. BODY-BASED PRACTICES

### 2.1 Progressive Muscle Relaxation - HIGH EVIDENCE

**Systematic Review:** 46 studies, 3,402+ participants
**Result:** Effective for stress, anxiety, depression
**SMD:** -1.35 for anxiety (COVID meta-analysis)
**Source:** [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38322293/)

### 2.2 Body Scan Meditation - HIGH EVIDENCE

**Large RCT:** n=2,239, 37 sites (Nature 2024)
**Effect Size:** d=-0.56 (largest of 4 exercises)
**Cortisol:** Declined in 8-week study
**Source:** [Nature Human Behaviour](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-024-01907-7)

### 2.3 NSDR/Yoga Nidra - MODERATE-HIGH EVIDENCE

**Findings:** Cognitive improvement, sleep +4%, cortisol reduction
**Caveat:** "Methodological weaknesses" in some studies
**Source:** [Wiley](https://iaap-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/aphw.12571)

### 2.4 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding - MODERATE EVIDENCE

**Base:** CBT/DBT clinical practice
**Studies:** Sahdra et al. 2023, Lang et al. 2022
**Source:** [Therapist Aid](https://www.therapistaid.com/therapy-article/grounding-techniques-article)

### 2.5 Dive Reflex - MODERATE EVIDENCE

**Meta-analysis:** Ackermann 2023
**Effect:** Moderate-large PNS increase during exposure
**Source:** [Psychophysiology](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/psyp.14183)

### 2.6 Butterfly Hug - MODERATE EVIDENCE

**Base:** EMDR protocol research
**RCTs:** Within EMDR-IGTP protocols
**Source:** [EMDRIA](https://www.emdria.org/resource/the-emdr-therapy-butterfly-hug-method-for-self-administered-bilateral-stimulation-iberoamerican-journal-of-psychotraumatology-and-dissociation/)

## 3. MOVEMENT PRACTICES

### 3.1 Walking/Mindful Walking - HIGH EVIDENCE

**Meta-analysis:** JMIR 2024, 44 RCTs
**Depression:** SMD -0.591
**Anxiety:** SMD -0.446
**Source:** [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11287235/)

## 4. SOUND-BASED

### 4.1 Binaural Beats - MODERATE EVIDENCE

**Review:** 12 studies (Applied Sciences 2024)
**Finding:** Better than control for anxiety/depression
**Caveat:** High heterogeneity, needs more RCTs
**Source:** [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/14/13/5675)

## 5. GRATITUDE & CONNECTION

### 5.1 Gratitude Interventions - MODERATE-HIGH EVIDENCE

**Meta-analysis:** 145 studies, 28 countries (PNAS)
**Result:** ~8% fewer anxiety symptoms, 7% fewer depression
**Source:** [PNAS](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2425193122)

### 5.2 Loving-Kindness Meditation - HIGH EVIDENCE

**RCTs:** 23+ studies
**C-METTA Effect Sizes:** PTSD d=-1.09, Guilt d=-2.85
**Source:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0272735824000540)

### 5.3 Self-Compassion - HIGH EVIDENCE

**Meta-analysis:** 56 RCTs, n=2,960
**Effect Size:** SMD=0.44 for depression
**Source:** [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10239723/)

## 6. COGNITIVE TECHNIQUES

### 6.1 ACT (Acceptance & Commitment Therapy) - HIGH EVIDENCE

**RCTs:** 1000+ worldwide
**Effect:** g=0.72 (65 studies, n=5,283)
**Depression:** SMD -0.69
**Anxiety:** SMD -0.64
**Source:** [PMC Overview](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10293686/)

### 6.2 STOP Technique - MODERATE EVIDENCE

**Base:** DBT/Mindfulness (Jon Kabat-Zinn)
**Components:** Stop, Take breath, Observe, Proceed
**Source:** [CBT LA](https://cogbtherapy.com/mindfulness-meditation-blog/mindfulness-stop-skill)
